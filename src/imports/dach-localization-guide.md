Ensure the website is fully optimized for the German-speaking audience in Germany, Austria, and Switzerland (DACH region). This includes translations, local keyword targeting, ensuring cultural relevance, and aligning with local search behaviors.

A. German Language Support:
	1.	Professional Translation:
	•	Translate all key content into German, ensuring accuracy and fluency. Avoid using machine translations (e.g., Google Translate) as they can create poor user experiences and negatively affect SEO. Use a professional translation service or native speakers who are experts in SEO to handle the translations.
	•	Content to Translate:
	•	All product pages (e.g., the homepage, try-on page, features page).
	•	Blog posts and any educational resources.
	•	FAQs and customer support pages.
	•	Legal pages (Terms of Service, Privacy Policy, etc.).
	•	CTA buttons (e.g., “Book a Demo” → “Demo buchen”, “Sign Up” → “Anmelden”).
	•	User-generated content (testimonials, reviews).
	2.	Cultural Adaptation:
	•	Cultural references and examples in your content may need to be adapted to resonate better with the German audience. For instance, replace references to US-specific brands, services, or trends with those that are more locally relevant (e.g., mentioning local fashion brands or influencers).
	3.	Local Communication Styles:
	•	Germans value formal tone in professional communications, so make sure your content is polite and formal where necessary (for example, using “Sie” instead of “du” when addressing the customer in German).
	4.	Localization of Date, Currency, and Units:
	•	Use German date format (DD/MM/YYYY).
	•	Ensure all currency symbols are in EUR (€) for German users.
	•	Provide metric units (centimeters, kilograms) rather than imperial units (inches, pounds).
	5.	Legal and Privacy Considerations:
	•	Adhere to German data privacy regulations (GDPR). Explicitly state your data protection measures in clear and user-friendly language to comply with local laws.
	•	Ensure that users can easily understand how their personal data is being handled. Include necessary opt-ins and legal disclaimers.
	6.	SEO-Optimized Translations:
	•	Translate meta tags (title tags, meta descriptions, alt text) into German, while keeping SEO in mind. For example:
	•	Title Tag (English): “AI-Powered Virtual Try-On Solution for Fashion Brands”
	•	Title Tag (German): “KI-basierte virtuelle Anprobe-Lösung für Mode-Marken”
	•	Meta Description (English): “Comify AI offers a seamless virtual try-on experience for fashion brands to increase engagement and reduce returns.”
	•	Meta Description (German): “Comify AI bietet ein nahtloses virtuellen Anprobe-Erlebnis für Mode-Marken, um die Interaktion zu steigern und Rücksendungen zu reduzieren.”

F. Technical SEO for Localization:
	1.	Hreflang Tags:
	•	Implement hreflang tags for language and region targeting:
	•	<link rel="alternate" hreflang="de-DE" href="https://www.comify.ai/de" /> for German-speaking users in Germany.
	•	<link rel="alternate" hreflang="de-AT" href="https://www.comify.ai/at" /> for Austria.
	2.	Regional URL Structures:
	•	Use a country code top-level domain (ccTLD) (e.g., www.comify.ai/de for Germany), or a subdirectory structure like comify.ai/de/ for German content.
	•	Ensure that the site navigation makes it easy for users to switch between languages and country-specific content.
